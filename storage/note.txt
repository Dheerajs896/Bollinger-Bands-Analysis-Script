What are Bollinger Bands?
Bollinger Bands are a volatility indicator developed by John Bollinger. They consist of three lines plotted around a price series:

The middle band is a simple moving average (SMA), typically over 20 periods.
The upper band is the SMA plus N standard deviations (default: 2).
The lower band is the SMA minus N standard deviations.

The formulas:-
Middle Band  = SMA(Close, 20)
Upper Band   = SMA(Close, 20) + 2 × StdDev(Close, 20)
Lower Band   = SMA(Close, 20) − 2 × StdDev(Close, 20)


How to interpret them:

When price touches or breaks the upper band → potentially overbought.
When price touches or breaks the lower band → potentially oversold.
Band squeeze (bands narrowing) → low volatility, often precedes a breakout.
Band expansion (bands widening) → high volatility in motion.
Statistically, ~95% of price action stays within the bands when using 2 standard deviations.
